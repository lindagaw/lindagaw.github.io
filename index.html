Ashley (Ye) Gao

My name is Ashley Gao, and I will be joining William & Mary as an Assistant Professor in August 2023.

Currently, I am a 4th year phd student at the Department of Computer Science at the University of Virginia. My advisor is professor John Stankovic. Before joining professor Stankovic's group, I obtained my B.S. in Computer Science and B.A. in Literatures of the World from the University of California, San Diego in 2017. I graduated with department honors of high distinction on my B.A., advised by professor Page duBois.

My research interest lie in the intersection of domain generalization, transfer learning, and how to use such techniques in applications in the cyber physical systems.

Domain Generalization

Domain generalization involves learning representations of the input data that capture underlying patterns common to multiple domains, and leveraging these representations to make predictions on new data..

Transfer Learning

Transfer Learning can help improve the performance of models on new tasks, by providing a head-start in learning the relevant patterns from the data.

Cyber Physical Systems

The seamless integration of sensors, controllers, and actuators with networked computer systems to monitor and control physical processes in real-time.
