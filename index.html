About Me

My name is Ashley Gao, a graduate student in the Department of Computer Science @ the University of Virginia. My studies focus on Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, and Human-Computer Interaction. I am working as a research assistant for and advised by Dr. John Stankovic.

I obtained my B.S. in Computer Science and B.A. in Literatures of the World, upon which I received department honors of high distinction, from UC San Diego in 2017. My honors thesis analyzes a recurrent theme in world mythology: Inanimate objects become sentient, and conscious beings become statues, flowers, etc. This gives me a unique perspective on machine learning, artificial life, and artificial intelligence.

Selected Computer Science Projects

A Comprehensive Product Rating System for Video Games Using Sentiment Mining

A review system to automatically generate a numerical rating for a video game based on users' review in English. We use the Aspect Segmentation Algorithm to categorize each sentence in a game review into an aspect and the contextual polarity algorithm to decide the sentiment the sentence is expressing. Based on the two algorithms, we can obtain the rating for each aspect and generate the final numerical rating based on the weights of the aspects.

Titanic: Predicting the Survival of a Passenger on the Titanic using Machine Learning Algorithms

Using machine learning techniques such Random Forests, K-Nearest Neighbors, SVM, and neural networks, this project predicts if a passenger is going to survive on the Titanic giving the passenger's information such as gender, age, social class. In the pre-processing process, the missing information is determined by the Expectation-Maximization Algorithm.
