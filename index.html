Welcome

My name is Ashley Y. Gao, and I am an Assistant Professor at William & Mary. I obtained my PhD in Computer Science from the University of Virginia (UVa), advised by Professor John Stankovic. My research interest lies in the intersection of Large Audio Language Models (Audio LLMs), Affective Computing, Natural Language Processing, and their application in smart healthcare.

Before joining Professor Stankovic's group, I obtained my M.S. in Computer Science at the University of Virginia, and my B.S. in Computer Science and B.A. in Literatures of the World at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD).

Projects

  • Domain-Generalized Audio Sensing — robust SER under distribution shift.

  • Audio Stress Detection for ADRD Caregivers — lightweight models linked to an mobile app to reduce ADRD caregiver stress.
