About Me

My name is Ashley Gao, a graduate student in the Department of Computer Science @ the University of Virginia. My studies focus on Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, and Human-Computer Interaction. I am working as a research assistant for and advised by Dr. John Stankovic.

I obtained my B.S. in Computer Science and B.A. in Literatures of the World, upon which I received department honors of high distinction, from UC San Diego in 2017. My honors thesis analyzes a recurrent theme in world mythology: Inanimate objects become sentient, and conscious beings become statues, flowers, etc. This gives me a unique perspective on machine learning, artificial life, and artificial intelligence. I even wrote a love letter for Artificial Intelligence, which can be found under the "Sidequests" category below, along with some other art pieces I created.
